Acosmism

The philosophical view that the world has no independent existence outside of God. The author contrasts Spinoza's absolute acosmism with Lurianic Kabbalah, where creation maintains a finite independent reality.

Aether

The spiritual medium or carrier substrate. The author maps the historical concept of aether to the spiritual energy fields that carry wave resonances between the human soul and the divine light.

Agnosticism

The claim that spiritual truths are unknowable. The author defines agnosticism as the logical dead-end of reason when cut off from the intuitive, spiritual frequency of faith.

Alchemy

Spiritual refinement. The author compares dietary kashrut laws to a form of spiritual alchemy, where eating acts to extract and refine holy sparks from raw biological matter.

Anthropomorphism

The error of assigning human form to God. The author utilizes Maimonides' warnings against anthropomorphism to emphasize that Kabbalistic terms (hands, eyes) are energy vectors, not physical shapes.
